Characters in Noli Me Tangere
Juan Crisostomo
Ibarra y Magsalin
or
Ibarra
  • The son of Don Rafael Ibarra and the betrothed of Maria Clara.
  • He studied abroad in Europe for 7 years. Upon his return to the Philippines, he sought to etablish a proper school there.
  • An idealistic young man, his education abroad inspired him to improve his country. He is outspoken about his beliefs and unafraid to fight for what he believes in.
Maria Clara de los Santos
or
Maria Clara
  • The supposed daughter of Kapitan Tiago and Doña Pia Alba. Her real father is Padre Damaso. She is also the betrothed of Crisostomo Ibarra.
  • She started studying at the Sta. Clara convent a little before Ibarra left for Europe.
  • She is a kindhearted, demure and graceful young woman. She is well-loved and admired by everyone around her.
Padre Damaso
or
Padre Garrote
  • He is Maria Clara's real father.
  • He is a Franciscan friar and the former town curate of San Diego.
  • He is a brash and arrogant individual who looks down on the Filipino people. As a friar, he often resorts to physical abuse towards his subordinates.
Padre Salvi
  • The predecessor of Padre Damaso as the town curate of San Diego.
  • He is a Franciscan friar and the town curate of San Diego.
  • Unlike his predecessor, Padre Damaso, he does not use physical abuse in order to get his way. Instead, he fines people as punishment for their misdeeds.
Don Anastasio
or
Pilosopo Tasio
  • He became an adviser to several people in San Diego and was also acquainted with Sisa. Years before, both his mother and his wife died in the same year.
  • He is an elderly scholar living in the town of San Diego.
  • He is thought of as a lunatic due to his odd ideas and frequent rambles. Despite this, some appreciated his knowledge, earning him the nickname Pilosopo Tasio.
Narsisa
or
Sisa
  • The wife of Pedro and the mother of Crispin and Basilio.
  • She is a generous mother to Crispin and Basilio. Aside from this, she was also kind to her husband Pedro despite his being abusive.
  • She is a caring but submissive woman. Despite having an abusive husband, she was kind to others and eventually earned the acquaintance of her neighbor, Pilosopo Tasio.
Crispin
  • The son of Sisa and the younger brother of Basilio.
  • He is a seven-year-old boy from a poor family, serving as one of the sacristans at the church of San Diego.
  • He is described as a fearful yet clever boy. He often speaks in riddles.
Basilio
  • The son of Sisa and the elder brother of Crispin.
  • He is a ten-year-old boy from a poor family, serving as one of the sacristans at the church of San Diego.
  • He is described as mature and brave. As the elder brother of Crispin, he took on a lot of responsibility and works tirelessly for their family.
Tenyente Guevarra
or
Miguel Guevarra
  • A close friend of Don Rafael Ibarra.
  • He is an elderly lieutant of the Guardia Sibil. As a good friend of Don Rafael Ibarra, he protected his son, Crisostomo Ibarra, after his return to the Philippines.
  • He is a loyal friend to Don Rafael Ibarra as he deeply respected the man before his passing. He also has a strong moral compass as to continue standing by Ibarra despite the potential dangers of this act.
Santiago de los Santos
or
Kapitan Tiago
  • The husband of Doña Pia Alba and the supposed father of Maria Clara. He once befriended Don Rafael Ibarra and Padre Damaso.
  • He is a businessman in San Diego. Aside from this, he, at some point, became gobernadorcillo of the town, earning him the nickname "Kapitan Tiago."
  • Despite being a man of high social standing, he was often looked down upon because of his submissiveness to the will of friars and other Spaniards. This behavior earned him the nickname Sacristan Tiago.
